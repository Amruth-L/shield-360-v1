Why Learn Self-Defense?

Self-defense teaches children how to stay safe, build confidence, and react calmly in difficult situations. Learning simple techniques can make a huge difference in everyday safety.

Basic Self-Defense Moves

1. Know Vulnerable Places

Learn where an attacker can be most vulnerable so you can protect yourself effectively.

2. Front Grab Escape

Practice techniques to free yourself if someone grabs your arm from the front.

3. Low Kick

A simple kick to the attacker’s legs can give you space to escape safely.

Groin kick

A groin kick is a swift strike aimed at an opponent’s lower abdomen for self-defense.

Escape with hands trapped

If your attacker comes from behind and traps your arms (this is similar to a bear hug, but you won’t be able to move as freely)

Escape from side headlock

When the attacker locks their arm around your head from the side, your first instinct should be to avoid getting choked.

Elbow strike

If your attacker is in close range and you’re unable to get enough momentum to throw a strong punch or kick, use your elbows.

Self-Defense Gadgets

Pepper Spray

Good weapon to escape from an attacker quickly and safely.

Note: Check local laws and age restrictions before buying or carrying defensive sprays.

Electric Taser

Delivers a high-voltage current to temporarily incapacitate an attacker.

Note: Check local laws and age restrictions before buying or carrying tasers.

Sharp-Pointed Keychains

Small, portable self-defense tool you can carry on your keys.

Note: Check local laws and age restrictions before buying or carrying defensive keychains.
